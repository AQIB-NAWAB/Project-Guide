# 1.4 — In scope / Out of scope

A project this size can sprawl forever. This section draws the line — so you build the right things deeply instead of a dozen things shallowly. Build everything *in scope* well; resist everything *out of scope*, however tempting.

## In scope — what you'll build

These are the real features of your platform, each a chapter (or part of one) in the course:

- **Communities** — create, browse, join, and leave communities.
- **Posts** — create, edit, delete, and view posts (text, link, and image) inside a community.
- **Nested comments** — threaded discussions where comments reply to comments, loaded and rendered efficiently.
- **Voting & ranking** — up/down votes on posts and comments, with **hot / top / new** ranking.
- **Feeds** — a per-community feed and a personalized **home feed** assembled from your joined communities, paginated.
- **Media** — image uploads on posts, stored in object storage, with thumbnails generated in the background.
- **Search & discovery** — search posts and communities; surface trending communities.
- **Real-time updates** — new comments and live vote counts that appear without a refresh.
- **Notifications** — in-app and email notifications (a reply to your post, etc.), delivered asynchronously.
- **Moderation** — reporting content, a moderator queue, remove/lock/pin, banning from a community, and an audit log.
- **Production concerns** — auth, authorization, rate limiting, caching, background jobs, logging, and a real **HTTPS deployment**.

## Out of scope — what you'll deliberately *not* build

Cut these without guilt. Each is a real product in its own right, and chasing them would bury the core:

- **Direct messages / live chat** — a whole separate real-time system.
- **Payments, awards, premium memberships, ads** — monetization is out.
- **A mobile app** — your front end is web; native apps are a different course.
- **Federation (ActivityPub)** — interoperating with Mastodon/the fediverse is a large, separate concern.
- **ML recommendations / "for you" algorithmic feed** — your ranking is rule-based (hot/top/new), not a machine-learned recommender.
- **Video hosting & transcoding** — images only; video pipelines are their own beast.
- **Rich WYSIWYG editing** — plain text (or simple markdown) for posts and comments is plenty.

If you finish early and want more, the **bar-raiser** at the end of the course is where you pick *one* of these (or something else) to add — on top of a solid foundation, by choice.

## The one-line user flow

Here's the whole product in a sentence, so you never lose the thread:

> **A visitor browses → signs up → joins a few communities → posts and comments → others vote and reply → moderators keep it healthy → it all ranks and updates live.**

Everything you build serves some part of that sentence. Next: what you should already know before you start.
