# 1.6 — The course outline

Here's the whole journey — four phases (one per week), each a set of **feature-chapters**, each chapter broken into 20–30 guided sub-chapters you'll work through in order. Read this once to see the shape of the climb; you'll come back to it as your map.

Each chapter is a milestone: when its checklist is green, a real part of your platform works.

## Phase 1 — Foundations *(Week 1: get it running, model it, build the core)*

| # | Chapter | What you'll build |
|---|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Introduction** *(you're here)* | Orientation: the product, how the course works, the plan |
| 2 | **Project setup & environment** | The full-stack skeleton: repo, TypeScript API, React app, Postgres & Redis in Docker, validated config, a health check |
| 3 | **Modeling the community** | The data model — users, communities, memberships, posts, comments (a tree), votes — with constraints, migrations, and seed data |
| 4 | **Communities** | Your first real feature, end to end: create, browse, and join/leave communities — with input validation and a clean error contract |
| 5 | **Posts** | Create, view, edit, and delete posts inside a community — backend and a React posts UI |

## Phase 2 — Identity & interaction *(Week 2: who you are, and what you can do)*

| # | Chapter | What you'll build |
|---|---------|-------------------|
| 6 | **Authentication** | Real auth: password hashing, register/login, access + refresh tokens, email verification, and the React auth flow |
| 7 | **Authorization & roles** | Members vs **community moderators** vs admins; owning your own content; scoping mod power to a community |
| 8 | **Comments — the thread tree** | Nested, threaded comments: modeling the tree, loading it without N+1, and rendering/collapsing deep threads |
| 9 | **Voting & ranking** | Up/down votes (no double-voting), denormalized scores, and the **hot / top / new** ranking algorithms |

## Phase 3 — Feeds, media & discovery *(Week 3: make it rich and fast)*

| # | Chapter | What you'll build |
|---|---------|-------------------|
| 10 | **Feeds** | The community feed and a personalized **home feed** from joined communities; pagination and the queries behind them |
| 11 | **Caching the hot paths** | Cache hot feeds and posts in Redis, and invalidate correctly when content or votes change |
| 12 | **Media in posts** | Image uploads straight to object storage via presigned URLs, with thumbnails generated in the background |
| 13 | **Search & discovery** | Search posts and communities, and surface **trending** communities |

## Phase 4 — Real-time, async, moderation & ship *(Week 4: bring it alive and deploy)*

| # | Chapter | What you'll build |
|---|---------|-------------------|
| 14 | **Real-time updates** | Live new comments and vote counts over WebSockets — no refresh needed |
| 15 | **Background jobs & notifications** | A job queue and worker; async media; in-app and email notifications; scheduled ranking recomputes |
| 16 | **Moderation tools** | Reporting, a moderator queue, remove/lock/pin, banning from a community, and an audit log |
| 17 | **Hardening & deploy** | Rate limiting, structured logging, graceful shutdown, security headers — then **deploy the whole stack over HTTPS** |
| 99 | **Closing** | Ship, document, raise the bar, and get ready to defend every decision |

## How the chapters connect

The order isn't arbitrary — each chapter **earns** the next:

> You can't post until there's a **community** to post in. You can't safely let *anyone* post until there's **auth**. Comments only matter once posts exist; **voting** only matters once there's content to vote on; **ranking** needs votes; **feeds** need ranking; **caching** matters once feeds are real; **real-time** brings the feeds alive; **moderation** keeps it all healthy; and then you **ship**.

You'll feel that staircase the whole way up. Last orientation section next — the habits to keep from day one.
