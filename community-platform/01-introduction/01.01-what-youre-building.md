# 1.1 — What you're building

You're about to build a **community discussion platform** — the kind of place where people start their own communities, post discussions, argue in threaded comments, and vote the best stuff to the top. If you've ever used **Reddit**, **Hacker News**, or a **Discourse** forum, you already know the shape of it: many small communities, each with its own posts, each post a living thread of replies, all of it ranked so the good rises and the noise sinks.

> *"The best way to learn to build software is to build software that people actually use."*

By the end of this course, yours will be **deployed, on the real internet, with people able to sign up and post.** Not a toy that runs on your laptop — a real, running product you can send someone a link to. You'll name it yourself; make it a community you'd actually want to hang out in.

## Why this project, specifically

A community platform is deceptively deep. On the surface it's "posts and comments." Underneath, it quietly demands **almost every hard problem in modern web engineering** — which is exactly why it's such a good thing to build:

- **The social graph** — who follows whom, who joined which community.
- **Tree-structured data** — a comment can reply to a comment can reply to a comment, ten levels deep. Storing and loading that *efficiently* is a genuine challenge.
- **Ranking** — "hot", "top", and "new" are real algorithms, not just `ORDER BY date`.
- **Feeds at scale** — a personalized home feed assembled from every community you've joined.
- **Real-time** — new comments and vote counts that update live, without a refresh.
- **Caching, moderation, async pipelines, media** — all of it, because a real community needs all of it.

Master this one project and you've touched the skills behind nearly every social product on the internet. That's a portfolio piece that opens doors — and, more importantly, makes you someone who can *build* one and *explain every decision in it*.

## What you'll have at the end

A full-stack application: a **TypeScript/Node API** backed by **PostgreSQL** and **Redis**, a **React** front end, media in **object storage**, **real-time** updates over WebSockets, background **workers** doing the slow work, and the whole thing **deployed over HTTPS**. Built by you, one guided step at a time.
