# 2.6 — Prime your thinking

Before you run `git init` and create folders, read **one** short piece on Node project structure so the module-based choice in 2.4 isn't only this course's opinion — it's a decision you've seen argued elsewhere too.

## Why this matters for absolute beginners

Reading fifteen minutes before `git init` feels slow when you want to code. It prevents **false confidence** — copying a layer-based tutorial and discovering at Chapter 11 that "products module" does not exist in your repo. The mandatory reads connect this course's module mandate to how Express routing actually works, so sub-chapter 2.11 is not your first time seeing `app.get` and middleware.

## Common beginner questions

**Q: Do I need to memorize the Express docs?**  
A: No — know where to look. Focus on basic routing, `express.json()`, and `Router()` — enough to mount `/health` and understand Chapter 6 POST bodies.

**Q: Can I skip the feature-vs-layer article if I trust the course?**  
A: Read it anyway — one paragraph in your learning log comparing both approaches proves you chose modules with eyes open, not because a checklist said so.

**Q: Should I read Mongoose or JWT now?**  
A: No — scattered reading derails focus. Trust chapter order; those topics get full chapters when your repo is ready.

**Q: What if I finish reading and still feel unsure?**  
A: Re-read 2.4–2.5 and complete the sanity-check sentences below. Wrong answers now are cheaper than wrong folders after 2.11.

## Mandatory read — before you scaffold the repo

Read **both** of the following (total time ~30–45 minutes):

### 1. Express application structure

**Topic to search:** *Express.js routing guide* — official Express documentation section on routing and middleware.

**Why now:** Chapter 2.11 mounts your first route. You should know what `app.get`, `app.use`, and `Router()` mean before you write them.

**After reading, you should be able to answer:**
- What does middleware do in the request pipeline?
- What's the difference between `app.listen` and defining routes on `app`?

### 2. Feature-based vs layered folder structure

**Topic to search:** *feature based vs layered folder structure node express*

**Why now:** You just chose module-based layout. This read shows you the alternative teams use and **when they switch**.

**After reading, you should be able to answer:**
- What problem does layer-based structure solve at small scale?
- Why do growing codebases often move toward feature folders?

## After reading — sanity check

Without opening notes, complete these sentences:

1. "In my repo, business logic for products will live in _____________."  
   → Expected: `server/src/modules/products/` (service + routes)

2. "The React app will get data by _____________."  
   → Expected: HTTP requests to the Express API (not direct DB)

3. "Database connection strings live in _____________."  
   → Expected: `server/.env`, not client

If any answer feels fuzzy, re-read 2.5 before 2.8.
