# 16.4 — Prime your thinking

Guest carts, login merge, and abandoned basket recovery are product decisions beyond MVP. This sub-chapter points at **cart merge on login** — you implement **authenticated cart only** for the gate, but mentors may ask what happens in a full marketplace.

---

## Why MVP skips guest cart

Guest cart sounds friendly ("browse without account") but adds:

- Session ids or localStorage sync
- Merge logic when guest becomes logged-in user
- Duplicate line handling
- More security review on anonymous writes

For learning **multi-vendor persistence and user scoping**, authenticated-only cart keeps the chapter focused. Chapter 17 can require login before add-to-cart — document that in README.

**You are not behind** if your app forces login to cart — that is the course gate.

---

## Interesting read — cart merge on login

Search topics:

- "e-commerce merge anonymous cart on login"  
- "Redis session cart vs database cart"  

**Typical pattern:**

1. Guest browses with session id — cart in Redis or localStorage  
2. User logs in — server merges guest cart into user cart  
3. Conflicts: same product in both — sum quantities or max  

**FreshMarket MVP:** No guest cart. Customer must login before POST /cart. Chapter 17 may require login to add to cart — document in README.
