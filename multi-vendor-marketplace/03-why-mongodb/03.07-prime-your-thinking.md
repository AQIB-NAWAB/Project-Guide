# 3.7 — Prime your thinking

Before you commit `docs/erd-draft.md`, read MongoDB's guidance on **data modelling** so your sketch reflects official vocabulary — one-to-one, one-to-many, embedding, referencing — not only this course's prose.

## Why this matters for absolute beginners

Course prose and official docs use slightly different words for the same ideas. Reading MongoDB's modelling introduction **once** means interview answers and your ERD use vocabulary employers recognize — "embedding for contained data," "reference when entity has independent lifecycle." You are not learning MongoDB from zero here; you are aligning your sketch with how the manual describes relationships.

## Common beginner questions

**Q: Do I need to read the entire MongoDB manual?**  
A: No — introduction sections on embed vs reference and the 16 MB limit are enough for this gate.

**Q: Why skim Mongoose if Chapter 5 implements schemas?**  
A: So your ERD field names match what `ref: 'Store'` and subdocument arrays will look like — fewer surprises when code meets diagram.

**Q: What if my ERD tool does not show embedded arrays well?**  
A: Describe embedded `orderItems` in prose below the diagram — many teams do. Clarity beats pretty pictures.
