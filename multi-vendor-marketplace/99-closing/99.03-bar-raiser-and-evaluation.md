## Viva question bank

Prepare concise answers — 30–60 seconds each. Practice out loud, not only in your head.

### Product & domain

1. Why multi-vendor grocery instead of single store?
2. What do `unit` and `stockQty` mean on products?
3. Walk through one checkout with two stores — how many orders? Who sees what?

### Security

4. How do you prevent vendor A editing vendor B's products?
5. Where do JWT secrets live? Why not in React?
6. What is IDOR and where did you test it?

### Data

7. Why MongoDB for this project? When would SQL win?
8. Why embed order lines with snapshots?
9. What happens to order display if vendor changes price after purchase?

### Performance & ops

10. How does catalogue caching work? When do you invalidate?
11. Why enqueue checkout notifications instead of sending email in the handler?
12. What runs in the worker process? What happens if worker is down during checkout?
13. What happens if Redis is down?
14. How did you deploy API, worker, and client? Where are production secrets?

### Beginner-friendly framing tips

- Start answers with **what the user experiences**, then **what the server does**.
- Use FreshMarket examples — "two stalls, two receipts" — not abstract jargon.
- "I don't know" + where you'd look (logs, Redis, worker terminal) beats confident wrong.

## Common beginner questions

**Q: I finished all chapters but failed bar-raiser on async explanation. Am I done?**  
Not yet. Re-run 21.15 demo, watch worker terminal during checkout, re-read 21.01 bridge. Understanding is graded.

**Q: Is viva scary?**  
It is a conversation about **your** project. CHALLENGES.md and learning logs are your cheat sheet — if you wrote them honestly.

**Q: What now?**  
Ship fixes until bar-raiser passes, then point portfolio at live URL + video. The repo is evidence; the viva proves it is yours.
