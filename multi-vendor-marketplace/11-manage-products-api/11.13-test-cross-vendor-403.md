# 11.13 — Test cross-vendor 403

## Why this step matters

Chapter 8 proved store isolation; this step proves **product** isolation — Vendor B cannot PATCH Vendor A's kale. Same curl template, new resource. The Chapter 11 gate depends on this negative test, not just happy-path create.

---

**After this step:** Full **vendor product lifecycle** and **isolation suite** recorded for learning log — the Chapter 11 proof.

---

## Prerequisites

- Vendor A and B — each with store and token  
- At least one product owned by A (`PRODUCT_ID_A`)  

---

## Test matrix (execute all)

### Lifecycle — Vendor A

| Step | Action | Expect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | POST product as A | 201 |
| 2 | GET /vendor/products as A | includes product |
| 3 | PATCH product as A | 200 |
| 4 | GET list — verify PATCH | updated fields |

### Isolation — Vendor B on A's product

| Step | Action | Expect |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | PATCH PRODUCT_ID_A as B | **403** |
| 6 | DELETE PRODUCT_ID_A as B | **403** |

### Cleanup — Vendor A

| Step | Action | Expect |
|---|---|---|
| 7 | DELETE PRODUCT_ID_A as A | 204 |
| 8 | GET list as A | product gone |

### Auth boundaries

| Step | Action | Expect |
|---|---|---|
| 9 | POST product no token | 401 |
| 10 | POST product as customer | 403 |
| 11 | GET /vendor/products as customer | 403 |

### No-store vendor (optional)

| Step | Action | Expect |
|---|---|---|
| 12 | POST product vendor without store | 404 |
