## In plain English

| Term | Simple meaning |
|---|---|
| **Checkout page** | The screen where the customer reviews their cart one last time and clicks "Place order" |
| **Order confirmation** | The "thank you" screen right after checkout — may show **more than one order** if items came from different stores |
| **Order history** | A list of past orders the customer can return to later |
| **Vendor order inbox** | The vendor's list of orders they need to pack and dispatch — only **their** store's orders |
| **Order status** | Where the order is in fulfilment — e.g. `pending` → `packed` → `dispatched` |
| **Snapshot fields** | Prices and names **frozen at purchase time** on each order line — so receipts never change when vendors edit products later |

## Think of it like a real grocery run

You filled your basket at two stalls in the market hall (multi-vendor cart). At the checkout desk you pay **once** (one checkout button). Behind the counter, the system prints **two receipts** — one per stall — because each shop fulfils and delivers separately (split orders).

Chapter 19 builds the screens for that moment:

- The customer sees both receipts on the confirmation page and in order history.
- Each stall owner sees only **their** receipt in their order inbox.
- When a stall marks "packed," the customer sees the badge update on refresh.

You already built the checkout desk logic in Chapter 18. This chapter builds the **receipts customers and vendors actually read**.

## Common beginner questions

**Q: Why another chapter after checkout API? Isn't the hard part done?**  
The hard **server** part is done. Real products need **screens** — customers do not curl. Chapter 19 is the frontend pair to Chapter 18, same pattern as cart API (Ch 16) then cart UI (Ch 17).

**Q: Do I need new backend routes?**  
Mostly no. You consume routes from Chapters 18–19. You may add `PATCH /vendor/orders/:id` for status updates in 19.10 if Chapter 18 did not include it.

**Q: Why show two orders on confirmation? Won't customers be confused?**  
One payment, multiple fulfilments — that is how multi-vendor marketplaces work. A short line of copy ("Each store fulfils separately") prevents confusion. Hiding the split would lie about what vendors see.

**Q: Can I use live product prices on order history?**  
No. Use snapshot fields (`nameAtPurchase`, `priceAtPurchase`) only. If you fetch live prices, you undo Chapter 18's immutability guarantee and break the demo when a vendor changes a price.

**Q: Chapter 20 is cache — can I skip straight there?**  
No. Chapter 19 gate is the **Week 3 phase demo**. Week 4 polish (cache, async, deploy) assumes commerce works end-to-end in the browser first.
